In the chart above, we see that annual avalanche death counts have generally trended higher over the years. Nationally, we see two primary jumps in the data--the first beginning in the mid-1970s, and a second jump higher in the 1990s. Since the 1990s, annual avalanche fatalities have trended fairly flat as a general trend. Some year-to-year variance is natural and expected due to varying weather and snowpack conditions each year.
